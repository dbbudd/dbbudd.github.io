Getting started

How to Get Started

Getting the playground onto your device takes under a minute.
Here's how it works.

1

Install Swift Playgrounds

Download Swift Playgrounds 4 for free from the App Store on iPad or Mac. It's the app you'll use to open and run all the code in this curriculum.

2

Tap Subscribe

Press the Subscribe in Swift Playgrounds button on this page. It opens a deep link (developer.apple.com/ul/sp0) that registers this feed directly in the app.

3

Open your library

The Geometry Playground appears in your Subscriptions section inside Swift Playgrounds. Tap it once to download the full playground file.

4

Stay up to date

Swift Playgrounds checks the feed periodically. When a new version is published a badge appears — tap once to update. No re-subscribing needed.

Standards alignment · IM1 · IM2 · IM3

Integrated Maths

The Integrated Maths sequence is a three-part high school mathematics programme that weaves algebra, geometry, and statistics into a unified curriculum — rather than treating them as separate subjects. Each course builds on the last, progressing from visual geometric reasoning through proportional thinking to analytic and algebraic geometry.

Geometry Playground is designed to sit alongside IM1, giving students a hands-on coding context for the geometric concepts they are studying in class. Because the curriculum emphasises reasoning and abstraction, it also lays the conceptual foundations that students will formalise in IM2 and IM3.

Chapter-by-chapter breakdown

G-CO.1

Know precise definitions of angle, circle, perpendicular line, parallel line, and line segment.

G-GPE.7

Use coordinates to compute perimeters of polygons and areas of triangles and rectangles.

Angle Measure

Classify angles as acute, right, obtuse, or straight and use degree measure to construct shapes.

Area & Perimeter

Calculate and compare area and perimeter of squares, rectangles, and triangles drawn in code.

A-SSE.1

Interpret parts of an expression — use a variable to represent a side length and substitute values.

G-CO.3

Given a rectangle, parallelogram, trapezoid, or regular polygon, describe the symmetries.

Reflective Symmetry

Identify and draw lines of symmetry in shapes drawn with multiple pens.

Area of Polygons

Decompose irregular polygons (diamonds, crosses) into rectangles and triangles to find area.

G-CO.3

Describe rotational symmetry of regular polygons — the loop count equals the order of symmetry.

Interior Angles

Derive and apply the formula (n−2)×180° for the sum of interior angles of an n-sided polygon.

Exterior Angles

Each exterior angle of a regular n-gon = 360°÷n — used directly as the loop turning angle.

Iteration Pattern

Recognise and express a repeating geometric pattern algebraically, then encode it as a loop.

G-CO.10

Prove theorems about triangles — angle sum, base angles of isosceles triangles.

Triangle by Sides

Classify triangles as scalene, isosceles, or equilateral using side-length comparisons in code.

Triangle by Angles

Classify triangles as acute, right, or obtuse using conditional tests on angle measures.

Quadrilaterals

Apply properties of parallelograms, rectangles, rhombuses, squares, and trapezoids to classify shapes programmatically.

Angle Relationships

Use supplementary, complementary, and vertical angle relationships as classification conditions.

F-IF.1

Understand that a function assigns exactly one output to each input — mirrored in parameterised drawing functions.

G-SRT.1

Verify experimentally the properties of dilations — scale a shape by changing a parameter and observe proportional change.

Similarity

Two shapes are similar if one is a dilation of the other — functions make this relationship explicit through a scale parameter.

Geometric Transformations

Encode translation, scaling, and rotation as functions with parameters for distance, factor, and angle.

G-MG.1

Use geometric shapes, their measures, and their properties to describe objects — model a house, tree, or car with polygons.

G-GMD.3

Use volume formulas for cylinders, pyramids, cones, and spheres — introduced through 2D analogues in the scene.

Composite Area

Calculate the total area of a suburban scene by summing the areas of its geometric components.

Decomposition

Break a complex real-world object into primitive geometric shapes — the key problem-solving strategy of the chapter.

Daniel is an Apple Professional Learning Specialist and Apple Distinguished Educator from Perth, Western Australia. At Hong Kong International School he spearheaded the design and development of an inclusive, modern Computer Science program by integrating it into the NCAS Media Arts Standards.

A strong advocate for the pedagogical integration of technology, Daniel focuses on the transformative potential of STEM education, robotics, and the evolving role of AI in shaping teaching and learning. As a speaker, author, and international educator, he has written extensively on coding, app development, and the impact of emerging technologies on education.

Daniel has delivered keynotes and workshops across Australia, South-East Asia, China, and the USA — inspiring educators worldwide to embrace innovation in their teaching practice.
